Daisy Bell: The first song sung by a computer

Author: Gatesana Vongphachanh

October 11th, 2022

In my MI 349 class there was a lab where I had to create a webpage about HAL 9000 from the movie "A Space Odesey". I'd never seen that movie before, so I was pleasantly surprised when the assignment contained the lyrics for "Daisy Bell"! One of my favorite things is the video of the first computer programmed to sing. The IBM 7094 sang the song "Daisy Bell" in an eerie yet sweet tone. (In my opinion) I really love hearing it sing and thinking about how excited the programmers must've been when their program was a success. I really adore it.

I found out about the singing computer from an animation by a YouTuber called Doobus Goobus who made the video for his partner (aw!). It's called "Little robot sings a little love song!"

The moment I saw the lyrics for my assignment, I rushed to YouTube to find a clip of HAL 9000 singing the song. HAL 9000 sings it a lot more slow and deep than IBM 7094, and it makes sense that the programmer would've chosen "Daisy Bell" for HAL to sing. It also has the opposite feel from IBM 7094's singing. Sadness. HAL 9000 is dying. There's a lot of interesting comments on the video I saw which makes me want to watch the movie. I'm not a big sci-fi person, though.
